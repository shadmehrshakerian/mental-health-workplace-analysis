# Mental Health in the Workplace: A Data-Driven Business Case

## Overview
Analysis of 3,866 employee survey responses across 4 years (2014–2018) examining the relationship between employer mental health support and workforce outcomes including treatment rates, productivity, stigma, and retention.

## Key Findings
- 72.4% of employees report mental health impacts their productivity
- Employer benefits increase treatment rates by 18 percentage points
- Supportive employer culture reduces stigma fear by nearly 8x
- Mental health disorder prevalence grew from 40% to 46% in just 2 years
- Estimated $2.72M annual cost of inaction per 1,000 employees
- $7.40 returned for every $1 invested in mental health support
